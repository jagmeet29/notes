Propositional logic

Propositional logic (PL) is the simplest form of logic where all the statements are made by propositions. A proposition is a declarative statement which is either true or false. It is a technique of knowledge representation in logical and mathematical form.

Examples:

  1. It is Sunday.
  2. The Sun rises from West (False proposition)
  3. 3+3= 7 (False proposition)
  4. 5 is a prime number.

Some sentences that do not have a truth value or may have more than one truth value are not propositions.

Examples:

  1. What time is it?
  2. Go out and Play
  3. x + 1 = 2

The above sentences are not propositions as the first two do not have a truth value, and the third one may be true or false.

Key Concepts

  • Proposition: A declarative statement that is either true or false, but not both.
  • Propositional Variables: Represented by lowercase letters (p, q, r, s) to denote propositions.
  • Compound Propositions: Formed by combining one or more propositions using logical connectives.
