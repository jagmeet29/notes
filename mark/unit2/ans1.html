Unit 2

I'll solve the recurrence relation shown in your notes: \( a_n - a_{n-1} - 2a_{n-2} = 0 \) with initial conditions \( a_0 = 0 \) and \( a_1 = 1 \).

Setting Up the Generating Function

Let's define the generating function:
\( G(x) = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} a_n x^n = a_0 + a_1 x + a_2 x^2 + \ldots \)

With our initial conditions, we get:
\( G(x) = 0 + x + a_2 x^2 + a_3 x^3 + \ldots = x + \sum_{n=2}^{\infty} a_n x^n \)

Deriving an Equation for G(x)

Multiply the recurrence relation by \( x^n \) and sum for \( n \geq 2 \):
\( \sum_{n=2}^{\infty} a_n x^n - \sum_{n=2}^{\infty} a_{n-1} x^n - 2\sum_{n=2}^{\infty} a_{n-2} x^n = 0 \)

Rewriting each sum:

  • \( \sum_{n=2}^{\infty} a_n x^n = G(x) - a_0 - a_1x = G(x) - x \)
  • \( \sum_{n=2}^{\infty} a_{n-1} x^n = x \cdot G(x) \)
  • \( \sum_{n=2}^{\infty} a_{n-2} x^n = x^2 \cdot G(x) \)

Substituting these into the equation:
\( (G(x) - x) - x G(x) - 2x^2 G(x) = 0 \)
\( G(x)(1 - x - 2x^2) = x \)
\( \Rightarrow G(x) = \frac{x}{1 - x - 2x^2} \)

Partial Fractions Decomposition

To decompose the fraction, we factor the denominator:
\( 1 - x - 2x^2 = -2(x - \frac{1}{2})(x + 1) \)

Then:
\( G(x) = \frac{-x}{2(x - \frac{1}{2})(x + 1)} \)

Using partial fractions:
\( \frac{-x}{2(x - \frac{1}{2})(x + 1)} = \frac{A}{x - \frac{1}{2}} + \frac{B}{x + 1} \)

Solving for A and B:

  • When \( x = \frac{1}{2} \), \( -\frac{1}{2} = 2A(\frac{3}{2}) \Rightarrow A = -\frac{1}{6} \)
  • When \( x = -1 \), \( 1 = -3B \Rightarrow B = -\frac{1}{3} \)

So:
\( G(x) = -\frac{1}{6(x - \frac{1}{2})} - \frac{1}{3(x + 1)} \)

Finding the Closed Formula

Using geometric series expansion:

  • \( -\frac{1}{6(x - \frac{1}{2})} = \frac{1}{3} \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} 2^n x^n \)
  • \( -\frac{1}{3(x + 1)} = -\frac{1}{3} \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} (-1)^n x^n \)

Combining the series:
\( G(x) = \frac{1}{3} \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \left[ 2^n - (-1)^n \right] x^n \)

So the closed-form expression is:
\( a_n = \frac{1}{3} \left[ 2^n - (-1)^n \right] \)

Verification

  • For \( n=0 \): \( a_0 = \frac{1}{3}[1 - 1] = 0 \) ✓
  • For \( n=1 \): \( a_1 = \frac{1}{3}[2 - (-1)] = 1 \) ✓
  • For \( n=2 \): \( a_2 = \frac{1}{3}[4 - 1] = 1 \) ✓
  • For \( n=3 \): \( a_3 = \frac{1}{3}[8 - (-1)] = 3 \) ✓

The formula satisfies the recurrence and initial conditions.

Final Answer:
\( a_n = \frac{1}{3} \left[ 2^n - (-1)^n \right] \)
