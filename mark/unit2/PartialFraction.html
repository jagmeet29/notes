Partial Fraction

Partial fraction decomposition is a technique used to break down a complex rational function into simpler fractions. This is particularly useful in calculus for integration. The basic idea is to express a given rational function as a sum of simpler fractions.

For example, consider the rational function:

$$ \frac{2x + 3}{(x - 1)(x^2 + 1)} $$

We can decompose it as follows:

$$ \frac{2x + 3}{(x - 1)(x^2 + 1)} = \frac{A}{x - 1} + \frac{Bx + C}{x^2 + 1} $$

where A, B, and C are constants to be determined.

Let's explore three alternative methods for partial fraction decomposition using the example:

Decompose $$ \frac{3x + 5}{x^2 + 5x + 6} $$

1. Substitution Method

Step 1: Factor the denominator:

$$ x^2 + 5x + 6 = (x + 2)(x + 3) $$

Step 2: Express as partial fractions:

$$ \frac{3x + 5}{(x + 2)(x + 3)} = \frac{A}{x + 2} + \frac{B}{x + 3} $$

Step 3: Multiply both sides by $$(x + 2)(x + 3)$$:

$$ 3x + 5 = A(x + 3) + B(x + 2) $$

Step 4: Substitute roots of the denominator:

  • Let $$ x = -2 $$: $$ 3(-2) + 5 = A(1) \implies -1 = A $$
  • Let $$ x = -3 $$: $$ 3(-3) + 5 = B(-1) \implies -4 = -B \implies B = 4 $$

Result:

$$ \frac{3x + 5}{(x + 2)(x + 3)} = \frac{-1}{x + 2} + \frac{4}{x + 3} $$

  • From $$ A = 3 - B $$, substitute into the second equation: $$ 3(3 - B) + 2B = 5 \implies 9 - 3B + 2B = 5 \implies -B = -4 \implies B = 4 $$
  • Then $$ A = 3 - 4 = -1 $$.

Result: Same as the substitution method.

3. Cover-Up Method (Shortcut)

Step 1: Factor the denominator:

$$ (x + 2)(x + 3) $$

Step 2: Find $$A$$ by "covering" $$(x + 2)$$ and substituting $$x = -2$$:

$$ A = \frac{3(-2) + 5}{(-2) + 3} = \frac{-6 + 5}{1} = -1 $$

Step 3: Find $$B$$ by "covering" $$(x + 3)$$ and substituting $$x = -3$$:

$$ B = \frac{3(-3) + 5}{(-3) + 2} = \frac{-9 + 5}{-1} = 4 $$

Result: Same as above.

When to Use Each Method:

  • Substitution: Best for distinct linear factors.
  • Equating Coefficients: Works for all cases (even repeated/quadratic factors).
  • Cover-Up: Quick trick for linear factors.

Final decomposed form:

$$ \frac{3x + 5}{(x + 2)(x + 3)} = \frac{-1}{x + 2} + \frac{4}{x + 3} $$
