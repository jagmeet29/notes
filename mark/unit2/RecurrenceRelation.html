Recurrence Relation

It is an equation that recursively defines a sequence where the next term is a function of the previous terms. For example, consider the inline math $c_{0} a_{0} + c_{1} a_{1} + c_2 a_{a-2} + \dots + c_k a_{a-k} = f(n)$ where $c_{0}, c_{1}, c_{3}, \dots, c_{k}$ are constants.

Examples:

  1. $s=\{2,2^2,2^3,\dots,2^n\}$ and the recurrence relation is $s_n=2s_{n-1}, \quad n\geq2,\quad s_1=2$.
  2. $s=\{3,7,11,15,19,\dots\}$ and the recurrence relation is $s_n=s_{n-1}+4,\quad n\geq2,\quad s_1=3$.
  3. $s=\{1,1,2,3,5,7,13,\dots\}$ and the recurrence relation is $s_n=s_{n-1}+s_{n-2},\quad n\geq3,\quad s_1=s_2=1$.

Find out whether there are linear recurrence relations:

  1. $2a_n + 3a_{n-1} = 3$
  2. $a_n - 7a_{n-1} + 12a_{n-2} = n \cdot 4^n$
  3. $a_n = a_{n-1} + a_{n-2}$
  4. $a_n = a_{n-1} \cdot a_{n-2}$
  5. $a_n \cdot a_{n-1} = a^2_{n-2}$

Ans:

1. yes
2. yes
3. yes
4. no (multiplication $a_{n-1}.a_{n-2}$)
5. no (multiplication $a_{n}.a_{n-1}$)

Order

It is the difference between the highest and the lowest subscript of $a$

Recurrence relation Order
$2a_n+3a_{n-1}=3$ 1
$a_n+7a_{n-1}-12a_{n-2}=0$ 2
$a_n+a_{n-1}+a_{n-2}+a_{n-3}=0$ 3
$a_n=2a_{n-1}+1$ 1

Degree

It is the highest power of n. The degree of recurrence relation is 1.

For example

  1. $a_n=2a_{n-1}+1$, the degree is 1
  2. $a_n=2a_{n-1}+3a_{n-2}+1$, the degree is 2
