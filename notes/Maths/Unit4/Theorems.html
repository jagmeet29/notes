Theorem 1

Handshaking Theorem

Let $G=(V,E)$ be an undirected graph with m edges. Then $$2m=\sum_{v \epsilon V}deg(v)$$

(Note this applies even if multiple edges or loops are present.)

How many edges are there in a graph with 10 vertices each of degree six?

Because the sum of the degrees of the vertices is 6 · 10 = 60, it follows that 2m = 60 where m is the number of edges. Therefore, m = 30.

It is possible to have $5,\ 4, \ 3, \ 7, \ 6$ degrees in a graph ?

No, Because $ \sum deg(v) = 5+4+3+7+6 = 25$ and $25=2e$ => $e=25\div 2=12.5$ and it is not possible to have 12.5 edges in a graph.

A graph $G$ has 21 edges, 3 vertices of degree 4 and all other vertices of degree 3. Find the number of vertices in $G$ ?

Let the total number of vertices be $n$. $$\begin{align*} \sum \deg(v) &= 3 \times \deg(4) + (n - 3) \times \deg(3) \\ 2e &= 3 \times 4 + (n - 3) \times 3 \\ 2 \times 21 &= 12 + 3(n - 3) \\ 42 &= 12 + 3n - 9 \\ 42 &= 3n + 3 \\ 3n &= 39 \\ n &= 13 \end{align*} $$

Find $n$, if a complete graph having $n$ vertices has 15 edges.

By degree of complete graph $K_n$ and Handshaking theorem.
$$\begin{align*} \\ 2\ e &= n (n-1)\\ 2 \times 15 &= n^2 - n \\ 30 &= n^2 - n \\ n &= 6,-5 \end{align*}$$ Ans $n=6$.

Theorem 2

An undirected graph has an even number of vertices of odd degree.

Proof

Let $V_1$ and $V_2$ be the set of even degree and the set of odd degree vertices respectively, in a graph $G=(V,E)$ with $m$ edges. Then $$2m=\sum_{v\epsilon V_1} deg(v)+\sum_{v\epsilon V_2} deg(v)$$

So, form the equation it can be seen LHS is multiple of 2 or even. So RHS must also be even, the first term is even as it is sum of even degree vertices. So, the second term must also be even. So, the number of odd degree vertices must be even.

Theorem 3

Let $G=(V,E)$ be graph with directed edges. Then $$\sum_{v \epsilon V} deg^-(v)=\sum_{v \epsilon V}deg^+(v)=|E|$$

Theorem 4

A simple graph is bipartite if and only if it is possible to assign two different colors to every vertex of the graph so that not adjacent vertex are assigned the same color.

Planar Graph Theorems

Theorem 1

Euler's Formaula

Let $G$ be a connected planar graph with $e$ edges and $v$ vertices. Let $r$ be the number of regions in a planner Representation of $G$. Then $r=e-v+2$.
