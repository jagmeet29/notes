Graph Terminology

Adjacent Vertices (or Neighbors)

  • If there is a edge $e$ that connects vertices $u$ and $v$, then $u$ and $v$ are said to adjacent.
  • This simply means that $u$ and $v$ are directly connected.

Incidence

  • When an edge $e$ connects both $u$ and $v$, we $e$ incident with $u$ and $v$.
  • Being "incident" means that the edge touches or connects the respective vertices.

Neighborhood of a vertex

The neighborhood of a vertex $v$ is written as $N(v)$, is simply the set of all vertices that are directly connected to $v$ by an edge. These vertices are called neighbors of $v$.

Example
$N(A) = \{B,C\}$ - A's neighbors are B and C $N(B) = \{A,D\}$ - B's neighbors
