Graph Terminology

Adjacent Vertices (or Neighbors)

  • If there is a edge $e$ that connects vertices $u$ and $v$, then $u$ and $v$ are said to adjacent.
  • This simply means that $u$ and $v$ are directly connected.

Loop

  • A loop is an edge that connects a vertex to itself. In other words, it is an edge that starts and ends at the same vertex.
  • Loops are often represented as a circle or a curve.
  • Loops can be found in both directed and undirected graphs.

Incidence

  • When an edge $e$ connects both $u$ and $v$, we $e$ incident with $u$ and $v$.
  • Being "incident" means that the edge touches or connects the respective vertices.

Neighborhood of a vertex

  • The neighborhood of a vertex $v$ is written as $N(v)$, is simply the set of all vertices that are directly connected to $v$ by an edge.
  • These vertices are called neighbors of $v$.
Example

$N(A) = \{B,C\}$ - A's neighbors are B and C
$N(B) = \{A,D\}$ - B's neighbors are A and D

Neighborhood of a set of vertices

  • The neighborhood of a set of vertices is all the vertices that connect directly to at least one vertex in your set.
  • Think of it this way: if you have a group of people, their collective neighborhood includes everyone who is friend with at lest one person in the group.
  • $N(A) =$ the union of all the neighborhoods of individual vertices in A.
Example

$N(\{A,B\}) = \{A,B,C,D\}$

Degree of vertex

  • It is the number of edges incident on the vertex.
  • Loop contributes twice to the degree of the vertex.
  • The degree of the vertex is denoted by $deg(v)$.

Insolated vertx

  • It is a vertex of degree zero.
  • It has no adjacent vertex.

Pendent

  • A vertex with degree one.
  • It is adjacent to exactly one vertex.

Directional Graph

  • In a directional graph, the edges are called arcs.
  • In a directional graph, the degree of a vertex is divided into two parts:
    1. In-degree $deg^-(v)$: the number of arcs directed towards the vertex.
    2. Out-degree $deg^+(v)$: the number of arcs directed away from the vertex.
  • Loop at a vertex contributes 1 to in-degree and 1 to out-degree.

Cycle

  • A cycle is a path in which the first and last vertices are the same. In other words, it is a closed loop in the graph.
  • A path that starts and ends at the same vertex, following the direction of edges

Adjacent Vertex in directed graph

In a graph $G$ having edge $(u,v)$. $u$ is said to be adjacent to $v$ and $v$ is said to be adjacent to $u$.

Initial & Terminating vertex

  • The vertex $u$ is called initial vertex of $(u,v)$ and $v$ is called the terminating vertex or end vertex of $(u,v)$.
  • The initial and terminating vertex of loop are same.

Source & Sink

  • A vertex with in-degree 0 is called a source.
  • A vertex with out-degree 0 is called a sink.

Parallel Edges

  • Two or more edges that connect the same pair of vertices are called parallel edges.
  • Parallel edges are not allowed in simple graphs.
